Lane Detection

Lane line detection is a computer vision technique used in advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) to identify the boundaries of traffic lanes on a roadway. It involves processing images or video frames captured by cameras mounted on the vehicle to detect lane markings, which are then used to guide lane-keeping and lane-centering functions, enhancing vehicle safety and driver assistance.

Traffic Sign Detection

Traffic sign detection systems work by capturing images of the roadway using vehicle-mounted cameras. The images undergo preprocessing to enhance quality and visibility of signs. Color segmentation helps in identifying regions with typical traffic sign colors. Shape detection algorithms then classify these regions based on common shapes of traffic signs (e.g., circular for speed limits). Feature extraction techniques like Histogram of Oriented Gradients (HOG) are used to capture detailed features of the signs. Machine learning models, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), are trained to classify these features into specific traffic sign categories. The system then compares detected signs with a database of known signs to ensure accuracy and provide real-time information to the driver or the autonomous system.
